# Week 1 · Introduction to the Module

**Slides:** [interactive deck](https://ahmad0maaitah.github.io/MANG3095/lectures/lecture01/)

## Learning objectives
- Understand what the UG dissertation is: 8,000 words (80%) plus a 2,000-word research proposal (20%), 30 CATS, 302 hours of independent study
- Know the suggested completion timeline, from supervisor allocation in early October to the 23 April submission deadline
- Know where support lives: the handbook on Blackboard, library subject guides, EDO data analysis tutorials, and the dissertation support sessions
- Manage the supervision relationship: the supervisor's role at the start, middle and end, and six habits for productive supervision
- Academic integrity for dissertations: plagiarism, collusion, external authorship, ethics approval and falsification
- Writing workshop: the three moves of an Introduction chapter (territory, niche, occupying the niche), the language and tenses of each move, and an annotated example introduction

## Interactive elements
1. **First-term action list** - a ten-step tick-off checklist mirroring the suggested timeline, with a live progress readout
2. **Check yourself quiz** - three self-marking questions on identifying the moves, ethics-first rules, and the proposal weighting
